What is the climate like in Estonia?

The climate in Estonia is situated in a transitional zone, located in the northern part of the temperate climatic belt and between the maritime and continental climates. Northern Europe and Estonia are positioned at a northern latitude but experience a temperate climate due to the warming influence of the North Atlantic. Estonia is a country with four distinct seasons. February is the coldest month in Estonia with a temperature of -5.7 degrees Celsius, while July is the warmest month with a temperature of 16.4 degrees Celsius. The average temperature across the country is 5.2 degrees Celsius. Estonia's climate is influenced by the North Atlantic Current and minimal Icelandic conditions. The country receives a significant amount of precipitation, with the annual evaporation being less than the total precipitation, making Estonia a humid country. The highest precipitation occurs in Sakala-Haanja and to the west of the Highland. Snowfall in Estonia lasts from mid-December to the end of March. The climate of the four seasons in Estonia varies distinctly from one another.
What is the climate like in spring?
Spring in Estonia is usually dry and mild. Snowfall can be observed until the end of March. Snowfall in March is replaced by thunderstorms in April. The hottest month of the spring season is May when the temperature reaches 20-30 degrees.
What is the climate like in summer?
In the summer season in Estonia, it is warm and mild, but on the Baltic Islands, it can be cool. On some days in April and May, the temperature can rise to 30-35 degrees. However, the average spring temperature is usually around 20 degrees. Although July is considered the hottest month, the highest recorded temperature in August was 35.6 degrees.
What is the climate like in autumn?
In the autumn season in Estonia, the weather is usually humid and windy. In mid-September, temperatures may occur, but thunderstorms won't be long in coming. Scandinavia often experiences hurricanes, and during the autumn period in Estonia's climate, there can be heavy rains. Flooding can lead to coastal inundation or winds from the shore, as the speed of some storms reaches 35 m/s. The onset of snowfall may be closer to the end of November.
What is the climate like in winter?
The winter season in Estonia corresponds to January and February. These are the coldest days. The climate is cold in the eastern part of Estonia, while the southern coast experiences milder temperatures. In Estonia, January and February are the coldest periods of the year, with temperatures dropping to -35 degrees Celsius. However, the lowest temperature recorded in the country is known to be -43.5 degrees Celsius. Snowstorms in Estonia typically accompany cyclones.
Where to go in bad weather in Tallinn?
The capital of Estonia is home to interesting museums, interactive exhibitions, art centers, cinemas, and theaters, as well as cozy cafes and restaurants. Of course, you can also warm up in saunas and swimming pools. There's a wide variety of options to choose from!
Where to go in bad weather in Tartu?
The university town of Tartu is the second-largest city in Estonia and the oldest in the Baltic countries. Tartu boasts several charming museums, such as the A. Le Coq Beer Museum, the Tartu Toy Museum, the University of Tartu Museum, and one of the country's largest, the Estonian National Museum. After exploring the exhibitions, you can immerse yourself in the lively atmosphere of bohemian bars, cafes, and restaurants.
